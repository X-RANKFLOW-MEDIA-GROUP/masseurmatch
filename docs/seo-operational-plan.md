# MasseurMatch SEO Operational Plan

## Strategic position

Primary message:

`The safest and most trusted premium male massage directory for direct connection.`

Operating rule:

- Win trust before scale.
- Win Google and AI discovery before brand familiarity.
- Use local intent pages to turn trust into compounding organic traffic.

## Priority order

### Phase 1: beat MassageFinder on trust perception

Goal:

- Make every public landing page feel more credible in the first 3 seconds.

Execution:

- Keep trust badges visible on cards and profile pages.
- Keep safety language linked from homepage, city pages, search, and profiles.
- Keep direct contact flow obvious without looking low-trust.

Core live pages:

- `/`
- `/search`
- `/safety`
- `/therapists/[slug]`

Primary keywords:

- `trusted male massage directory`
- `verified male massage therapist`
- `safe male massage near me`
- `male massage directory with verified profiles`

### Phase 2: steal local searches from MasseurFinder

Goal:

- Own city plus intent combinations with better UX and better trust framing.

Execution:

- Publish city pages first.
- Publish city plus segment pages second.
- Publish city plus segment plus keyword pages third.
- Keep comparison links and safety links inside local pages.

Core route structure:

- `/[city]`
- `/[city]/verified-profiles`
- `/[city]/male-therapists`
- `/[city]/lgbtq-friendly`

### Phase 3: capture new discovery before RentMasseur

Goal:

- Rank for the searches a user makes before they know any brand.

Execution:

- Expand intent pages with trust-first copy.
- Add city guides and comparison pages to capture top and mid funnel queries.
- Add stronger empty-state routing so every low-inventory page still pushes users deeper.

- `gay massage dallas verified`

### Phase 4: neutralize ProMasseurs with verification plus scale

Goal:

- Match trust language, but back it with more indexable pages and clearer UX.

Execution:

- Keep verification language tied to real badge states.
- Expand city coverage faster than profile-only competitors.
- Use compare pages to frame MasseurMatch as the cleaner main destination.

Core route structure:

- `/compare`
- `/compare/masseurmatch-vs-masseurfinder`

- `/compare/masseurmatch-vs-massagefinder`

### Phase 5: crush the long-tail cluster

Goal:

- Take search volume from smaller niche directories with cleaner local pages and better editorial consistency.

Execution:

- Add more city guides.
- Add suburb and neighborhood pages where inventory supports it.
- Add more compare pages for smaller competitors.

## On-page SEO rules

Every city or intent page should include:

- A trust-led H1
- A short intro with city plus intent language
- Internal links to adjacent segment or keyword pages
- A listing block or a strong fallback CTA block
- FAQ schema
- ItemList schema when listings are present
- Breadcrumb schema
- Clear canonical path

Copy pattern:

- Lead with trust
- Mention city early
- Mention session format and specialties naturally
- Avoid overusing brand terms
- Keep the page useful even when inventory is light

## Internal linking rules

- Homepage links to search, safety, compare, top cities, top intents
- Search links to safety, compare, and city pages
- City pages link to segments, keyword pages, and top competitor pages
- Segment pages link to keyword pages and back to city pages
- Compare pages link to pricing, register, compare hub, and related comparison pages
- Guides link to city pages, safety page, and relevant compare pages

## Schema rules

- Homepage: `CollectionPage` plus `ItemList`
- City pages: `CollectionPage`, `BreadcrumbList`, `ItemList`
- Profile pages: `ProfilePage`
- Compare pages: `Article` plus FAQ schema
- Guides: `BlogPosting` or `Article`
- Safety page: `FAQPage`
